The book came out yesterday, and that morning I awoke to this stunning NPR [review](http://www.npr.org/2015/09/29/443410272/last-song-is-a-beautifully-orchestrated-fantasy-debut) of *Last Song Before Night:* 

**“Myers’ depiction of Tamryllin and the land it inhabits is shadowy and lush, a tapestry of gossamer wonders as well as theocratic oppression and brutality. But the core of *Last Song’*s strength is its characters. Bound by enmities, rivalries, lust, sacrifices, and ancient tragedies, the novel’s sizeable cast forms a dizzying chemistry…*Last Song Before Night* is about music, but it’s also a work of music itself: Lyrical, dynamic, and winningly melodic.”** 

Complete [NPR review of *Last Song Before Night* by Ilana C. Myer](http://www.npr.org/2015/09/29/443410272/last-song-is-a-beautifully-orchestrated-fantasy-debut)

There are also lovely reviews at [Tor.com](http://www.tor.com/2015/09/29/last-song-before-night-archetypes-epic-songs/), [SF Signal](http://www.sfsignal.com/archives/2015/09/last-song-night-ilana-c-myer-sumptuous-symphony-music-magic/), and [Bookwormblues](http://www.bookwormblues.net/2015/09/22/last-song-before-night-ilana-c-myer/), and an exceptionally thoughtful review at [Galleywampus](http://galleywampus.com/review-and-giveaway-last-song-before-night-by-ilana-c-myer/). Paul Weimer at SF Signal, who reviewed my book, also [interviewed](http://www.sfsignal.com/archives/2015/09/ilana-c-myer-author-last-song-night-fantasy-debut-intersection-art-power/) me, which was a first. I’ve interviewed many authors, but had never been interviewed, myself!

**Word of mouth counts for a lot.** If you read *Last Song Before Night* and enjoy it, please consider leaving a review at Goodreads, Amazon, or wherever is most convenient for you. I am excited for my book to be out in the world and in your hands at last.

Oh, here I am autographing copies at the Union Square Barnes & Noble.
